mo That New Story I've wanted to write

Alma wakes up suddenly, eyes wide open, then slowly closes them and rests her head on the pillow, still, for a few more seconds. She draws a long, generous yet tired breath, and tells herself: "Only an arm or a leg has to move out of bed. Don't worry, the rest will follow."
And that's what she does. She pulls a forearm away from the beds' gravity pull. Slowly, the rest of her body follows her arm. Her head tilts as she gathers muscles to support her sitting up. Her horizon shifts.
Then she speaks, out loud: "Hello, Tuesday June 4 2019."
